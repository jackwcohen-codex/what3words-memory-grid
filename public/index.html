How to play

Each round starts with three study squares. They flash on the map one at a time.

When a square flashes, its what3words address appears next to the square and again in the right-hand panel. Try to remember both the address and where the square was.

After the study squares finish, one of those addresses appears again in the right-hand panel. Click the grid square where you think you saw it.

Your base score depends on how many grid squares away your click is. Exact is 100, 1 square away is 80, 2 away is 60, 3 away is 40, 4 or 5 away is 20, and further away is 0.

Difficulty changes the size of the map area. Beginner is the smallest and easiest, then Easy, Medium, Hard, and Lethal get larger and harder.

Difficulty also multiplies your score: Beginner x1, Easy x2, Medium x5, Hard x10, and Lethal x20.

Study pace changes how long each square stays visible. Relaxed is slowest, Normal is faster, and Quick is fastest.

Study pace also multiplies your score: Relaxed x1, Normal x3, and Quick x5. So an exact match is 100 points on Beginner and Relaxed, but much more on harder and faster settings.
